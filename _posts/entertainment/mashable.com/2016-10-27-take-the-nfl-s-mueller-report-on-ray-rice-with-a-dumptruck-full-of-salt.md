On Sept. 19 — as the NFL's Ray Rice scandal was at its apex and calls for his job were at their loudest — NFL Commissioner Roger Goodell stepped behind a lectern in New York City and tried to begin the league's long public relations rehabilitation. Visibly attempting to be earnest with a capital "E," Goodell said he and the league that rakes in cash by the billions had made mistakes. But they were prepared to examine and atone for those mistakes, Goodell said.
